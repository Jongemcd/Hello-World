Executive summary

Of the 1,355.5 hours of HU3 downtime logged from Mar 11 to Jul 16, 2026, 1,069.8 hours (78.9%) reflects a genuine equipment or process failure — a much cleaner split than the packaging line, where guard interlocks alone matched the real failure total. Here the noise is smaller and more concentrated: Advisory / Maintenance Notice messages (recurring UPS-battery check reminders and an undescriptive "SR Dependency list" flag) are 9.6%, guard/e-stop interlocks are 7.9%, and operator/procedural plus pre-warning messages make up the remaining 2.6%.

The cleaned Pareto is dominated by a single chronic issue: "Main air valve basic machine pressure not present" accounts for 590.4 hours — 55.2% of all real technical downtime, and 43.6% of every hour of downtime logged on this machine, cleaned or not. At roughly 3.7 minutes per occurrence across 9,533 events, this isn't sensor chatter (unlike a similar-sounding issue found on the packaging line) — it's a sustained, recurring air-supply problem and the clear #1 priority below.

Classification method (so you can adjust it)

HU3's fault codes already carry a severity prefix — Warning_, Alarm_, or Fault_ — assigned by the control system itself. That prefix is used as the default signal (Warning → advisory/noise, Alarm/Fault → real), but content-based overrides run first and win regardless of prefix, since the same physical condition can be logged under any of the three severities depending on how long it persisted.
