Methodology & caveats

Linking method: the master log is a change/event log — each row's duration runs until the next row (capped at 4 hours to stop rare multi-day logging gaps from skewing totals; 16 of 361,446 rows were affected). For every master row where FirstFaultText is @Fault_0081 or @Fault_0085 and Original_OEE_MachineStatus = 0 (stopped), we take that row's active window and pad it ±30 seconds to absorb clock/PLC-scan skew between the two control systems, then pull every child alarm-stack row whose timestamp falls inside that padded window.
Coverage: 13,596 @Fault_0081/@Fault_0085 stoppages occurred across the full master log; 1,421 of them predate the child log's Jan 22, 2026 start and can never be linked. Of the remaining 12,175, 88.6% have at least one child alarm captured in their window.
Reading "No product from upstream machine": this is the child's own alarm for "master isn't feeding me" — it is the mirror image of the master's "waiting for Schubert" fault, not an independent root cause. When it dominates a window, treat it as confirmation the two machines are in a synchronized hand-off stall, and look to the next-ranked alarm (overflow container, magazine refill, Sercos bus, compressed air) for the actual trigger.
